Matter Dictionary

物质词典

All matter in the universe is built from the same fundamental particles. These symbols (0.2x) describe the building blocks of reality — from electrons to atoms to ions. 宇宙中的所有物质都由相同的基本粒子构成。这些符号(0.2x)描述了现实的基本构件——从电子到原子再到离子。

Expressing Atoms

原子表达方式

Structure: 0.2 + 0.21 + electrons + 0.22 + protons + 0.23 + neutrons

0.2 0.5 0.21 0.5 [electron count] 0.5 0.22 0.5 [proton count] 0.5 0.23 0.5 [neutron count] 0.5 0.9

Matter, electrons=[n], protons=[n], neutrons=[n].

Any neutral atom has equal electrons and protons. The proton count (atomic number) uniquely identifies the element. 任何中性原子的电子数和质子数相等。质子数（原子序数）唯一确定元素。

Expressing Ions

离子表达方式

Ions have unequal electrons and protons. The difference indicates the charge. If electrons < protons, it's a positive ion (cation). 离子的电子数和质子数不相等。差值表示电荷。如果电子数 < 质子数，则为正离子（阳离子）。

Nuclear Reactions

核反应

Nuclear reactions — like the fusion occurring in every star — are more universal than chemical reactions. They depend only on the fundamental forces of nature, not on local environmental conditions. 核反应——就像每颗恒星中发生的聚变——比化学反应更具普遍性。 它们只依赖于自然界的基本力，而不是局部环境条件。

"We are all made of star-stuff."
— Carl Sagan

Every atom in your body was forged in the heart of a dying star billions of years ago. The carbon in your cells, the iron in your blood, the calcium in your bones — all were created in stellar furnaces and scattered across the cosmos in supernova explosions. Any civilization receiving our message shares this cosmic ancestry.

你身体中的每一个原子都是数十亿年前在垂死恒星的核心中锻造的。 你细胞中的碳，你血液中的铁，你骨骼中的钙—— 都是在恒星熔炉中创造的，并在超新星爆炸中散布到整个宇宙。 任何接收我们信息的文明都共享这一宇宙血统。
